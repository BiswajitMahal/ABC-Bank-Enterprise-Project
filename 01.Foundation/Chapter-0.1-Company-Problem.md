# Chapter 0 – Why Do Companies Move to Cloud?

## Objective

Understand the business problems that led organizations to adopt Cloud Computing before learning Azure services.

---

## Business Scenario

ABC Bank plans to launch a new Internet Banking Platform.

Initially, the company wants to host the application inside its own Data Center.

Before choosing Azure, the engineering team must understand the challenges of managing infrastructure on-premises.

---

## What is a Server?

A server is simply a computer designed to provide services to other computers over a network.

Examples:

* Web Server
* Database Server
* File Server
* Application Server

---

## What is a Data Center?

A Data Center is a facility that contains:

* Servers
* Storage
* Network Switches
* Routers
* Firewalls
* UPS
* Cooling Systems
* Physical Security

---

## Challenges of On-Premises Infrastructure

* High Hardware Cost
* Hardware Maintenance
* Limited Scalability
* Power Failure Risk
* Cooling Requirements
* Disaster Recovery Complexity
* Long Procurement Time

---

## Business Problem

During festivals, the number of users increases significantly.

The existing servers cannot handle the additional traffic.

Result:

* High CPU Usage
* Memory Exhaustion
* Application Downtime
* Business Loss

---

## Why Cloud?

Cloud Computing allows organizations to consume infrastructure on demand without purchasing and maintaining physical hardware.

Benefits:

* On-demand Infrastructure
* High Availability
* Elastic Scalability
* Faster Deployment
* Pay-as-you-go Pricing
* Managed Services

---

## Key Learnings

* Every application needs infrastructure.
* Traditional infrastructure is expensive to build and maintain.
* Cloud solves business problems rather than simply providing virtual machines.

---

## Summary

Before learning Azure, it is important to understand why companies migrate to the cloud. Every cloud architecture begins by solving a business problem.

* A data center is a facility that hosts servers, networking devices, storage, and power infrastructure.
* Cloud solves business problems rather than simply providing virtual machines.
